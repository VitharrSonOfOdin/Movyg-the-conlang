Conjugation of verbs

Every verb in Móvyg has a letter e in it, and this e gets affected by the conjugation. If a verb has more than 1 e, only the first one changes. I will use "jet" (to eat) as an example.

Present: Singular:
First person: e → a (jet → jat)
Second person: e → ar (jet → jart)
Third person: e → o (jet → jot)

Past:
First person: e → a + m at the end (jet → jatm)
Second person: e → ar + m at the end (jet → jartm)
Third person: e → o + m at the end (jet → jotm)

In past you just add m at the end
Future:
First person: e → a + m at the end + the word "to" at the beginning (jet → to jatm)
Second person: e → ar + m at the end + the word "to" at the beginning (jet → to jartm)
Third person: e → o + m at the end + the word "to" at the beginning (jet → to jotm)
So just past but you add “to” in the beginning.
Plural:
In plural you just add k at the end, like:

gar jatk
kart rarmk
þyn to jotmk
or, a more formal option, in past and future forms only, i looks like:
Past:
Gar jatkym
Kart jartkym
Þyn jotkym

Instead of k being at the end, it's after the verb core and after that there's ym, not m. In future you still just add to in the begging

Counting

If you want to make 13 — 19, you take “dylo” and add the desired number at the end, so 13 is dylomu,19 is dylogor. If you want to say a big number like, 45731 you just say it as so: kyrdylbec ondylo ose odylo mudyl yw (literally: forty-five thousand seven hundred thirty one) (45 000, 700, 31.) Like in English.

Questions

When you want to ask a question in Móvyg, you add a question word in the beginning. All the questions are enclosed in ¿ ?.

Subjectless sentences

You probably know the difference between fuck you and you fuck. In Móvyg there's this difference too. I think it must be because in fuck you there's no subject, and in you fuck the subject is you. You can create subjectless sentences in Móvyg just by not telling the subject.

Comparison of adjectives

Every adjective ends with t and this t gets affected when you make it a higher level. In English, it's done by suffixes er and est. In Móvyg it's done by suffixes and prefixes, but In Móvyg it's tvyg for middle form and for the high form, you add tvyg at the end and gor in the beginning
